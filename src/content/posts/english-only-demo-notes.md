This is an intentionally English-only demo article.

Its main job is not to add more documentation. Its job is to prove one narrow but important behavior: **if a translation does not exist, the theme does not generate a synthetic page for the other locale.**

## Why this kind of post matters

Real bilingual blogs rarely translate every single piece of content. In practice, some posts remain locale-specific:

- announcements aimed at one audience
- local notes or reminders
- articles whose context is too language-specific to mirror cleanly

If the system tries to force a mirror page for those posts, it usually makes routing, SEO, and reader expectations worse.

## What to verify

This post exists on the English site only. Therefore:

- the English route is generated normally
- the Chinese route is not fabricated
- locale switching should not pretend a Chinese equivalent exists

That boundary is an important part of the theme’s bilingual publishing model, which is why this short article belongs in the demo at all.
